Instructions

Medical Annotation Job

Overview

Help us determine if the person has pneumonia in the images we provide.

Steps

  1. Examine the image.
  2. Check the appropriate box (no, yes, unknown) for pneumonia in the lungs by looking at the patient's chest x-ray images.
    1. If you see an abnormal black tone by looking at the chest x-ray images, check the yes box.
    2. If you don't see an abnormal black tone by looking at the chest x-ray images, check the no box.
    3. If you are not sure if the patient has pneumonia by looking at the chest x-ray pictures, check the unknown box.
  3. Check the appropriate box according to your design.
    1. If you are sure about your design, check the High box.
    2. If you are not sure about your design, check the Low box.

Rules Tips

Rules:

  • Only look for images of chest x-rays.
  • A normal, healthy image will depict clear lungs without any areas of abnormal cloudiness/opacity; there may be structured, web-like vasculature in the lungs but otherwise that area should be clear. In healthy images, you are also more likely to see a diaphragm shadow.
  • A pneumonia image may include a few things: areas of cloudiness/opacity in several concentrated areas or one large area. You may also see a general pattern of opacity that obscures the structure of the lungs, heart, and diaphragm.
  • If you are uncertain, do not take a chance, please choose the unknown button.
  • In any case, do not select two buttons / options.
